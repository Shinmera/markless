\definesubsection{Dashes}
\defineidentifier{em-dash}{--}
\definetextualcomponent{em-dash}{display: em-dash} \\

Em-dash is a \g{entity inline directive}. If the \g{document} does not have direct support for em-dashes, a fallback character may be used when appropriate instead. In unicode encoded documents, this should be \unicode{2014}. \\

\begin{examples}
  \example{A game -- or gamble, if you will.}{A game --- or gamble, if you will.}
\end{examples}
